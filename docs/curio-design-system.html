Curio Design System

"Chill Cartography for hobbies." A calm paper foundation with tactile, alive motion. Warm parchment, faded golden accents, midnight-teal ink, a rust stamp, and one plum spark reserved for joy. Every token here is the same one this page renders with.
01

A brand-owned, low-saturation palette — the visible form of the anti-doomscroll pitch. It's distinctive in a category of screaming apps. The rule: calm everywhere, one bright spark (plum/gold) reserved only for celebration.

Both themes are token-level. Dark mode is a deep forest-paper world (this doc is rendering it if your device is dark) — atmospheric and on-brand for evening use. Contrast is a known risk of a muted palette — every text/background pair must clear WCAG AA (4.5:1 small, 3:1 large) at its real size, and meaning is never carried by color alone.
02

Typography

Three roles: a serif display (warmth, editorial calm), a humanist sans for body (legibility at arm's length), and a monospace for data/labels (the "catalog" texture). The round-3 fix: a hard size floor, because the charming mono was getting pushed illegibly small.

Rules: nothing below 11px, ever; monospace floors at 12px (it reads smaller than sans); mono is for eyebrows/tags/stats only, never body copy; headings get text-wrap:balance; running text stays near 65 characters wide.

06

Motion & tactility

The most important section. Static, the calm aesthetic risks feeling dead to a young audience. Motion is how we prove it's calm, not lifeless. Spend the energy budget on three signature moments — everything else stays quiet.

Signature momentMotionHaptic / soundFeel
The Stamp
rating a challenge		Press-down + slight rotate + settle, the number "inking" in rustA firm thunk + a soft paper-press soundThe app's defining moment — like stamping a passport. Satisfying enough to do for its own sake.
The Deal
dealing a new hand		Cards slide in and settle with a real card's weight and overshoot-free easeA soft shuffle whisperTactile, physical, "these are real cards."
The Landing
completion → cabinet		The new specimen token eases into its drawer slot; a single plum/gold glintA gentle chime + light hapticThe reward. The one moment the joy-spark color appears.
Motion principles: calm easing (no springy bounce-fest — that reads as childish/AI), short durations (150–350ms), one thing moving at a time, and always respect prefers-reduced-motion (fall back to a simple cross-fade + the haptic). Never slot-machine energy — this is a passport stamp, not a jackpot.
07

Do & don't

Do
  • Keep the calm paper foundation — it's the brand's biggest asset.
  • Spend energy on motion & the three signature moments.
  • One filled primary button per screen; CTAs in the thumb zone.
  • Line icons, consistent stroke, everywhere in chrome.
  • Design empty/offline/loading states in-voice, paper-toned skeletons.
  • Show what you've collected, never what's missing.
Don't
  • Let it feel like a museum — static + all-muted = dead.
  • Ship text under 11px, or monospace body copy.
  • Use emoji in the app's own chrome (only in user reflections).
  • Stack two competing filled CTAs (the Today-screen slip).
  • Show empty ghost cabinet slots (smuggles back streak-anxiety).
  • Use bouncy/springy or slot-machine motion — stay premium-calm.
The design north star: "calm and alive." Nobody in the daily-habit / hobby space has pulled off both — they're either loud (Duolingo) or sterile. Curio's whole visual bet is that a warm paper world with three perfect tactile moments can feel more premium and more human than any confetti explosion. The foundation is ~80% there; the remaining 20% is motion and craft, not redesign.
